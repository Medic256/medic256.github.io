🏆 Leading by Example at a National Taekwondo Qualifier

Posted July 12, 2025 • By Enrique Becwar

Over the weekend I competed in a national‑qualifying Taekwondo tournament in Chicago and earned:

  • 1st place – Traditional Forms (ITF Tul Yoo‑Sin)
  • 3rd place – Weapons Forms (Sai – Chun Kuhn Taekwondo)

While I’m proud of those results, the true highlight wasn’t the podium—it was a lesson in resilience that unfolded after the competition.

A Community That Feels Like Family

The venue felt less like a sports hall and more like a long‑lost family reunion. Coaches, competitors, and parents cheered every athlete—regardless of school or region. There were no participation trophies, only 1st, 2nd, and 3rd places—but the atmosphere was fueled by the same energy you find in dojangs worldwide: respect, humility, and shared pride.

The Board That Wouldn’t Break—Until It Did

One moment crystallized the spirit of the day. A young competitor—maybe five years old—stepped up for her board‑breaking event. She shattered the first two boards but the final station refused to yield. Instead of accepting defeat, she made a quiet decision: she would break that board before leaving.

After the awards were handed out, she tracked me down on the crowded floor and asked if I would hold the board one more time. We tweaked her stance, adjusted her hip rotation, and on her third attempt she snapped through the board cleanly.

The look on her face—equal parts pride, relief, and pure joy—was the real victory of the weekend. In that instant, she learned that perseverance, not plaques, defines success.

Why I Show Up

This experience reminded me that martial arts is ultimately about building people, not just techniques. Every kick and every board is a lesson in resilience. That young martial artist taught me as much as I taught her:

If you keep looking for a way forward, the breakthrough will come—sometimes literally.

Moments like these fuel my passion to teach, mentor, and keep sharpening my own craft—whether on the mat or in cybersecurity, where perseverance and precision matter just as much.
